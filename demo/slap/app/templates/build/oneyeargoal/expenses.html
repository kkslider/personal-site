Fixed Costs

Yes, you are an entrepreneur, but you also need a paycheck. Let's do a little work and figure out what you'll need to pay yourself. Take a moment to write down all of your personal expenses. And be honest - this exercise only works if you don't hold back. Do not fudge the numbers!

These are the costs of doing business. Remember, these are not your variable costs associated with your units of sale. These are your hard fixed costs; the ones that you are liable for even if you do not open up for business that year! We will explore your variable costs on the next page, but for now concentrate hard on capturing all of your fixed costs (e.g. rent, insurance, payroll, etc.). These should also be forward looking. For example: If you expect rent to go up by $50 for the coming year, input the price including the increase.

Again, be honest with these numbers and spend time thinking of everything. This only works if you are honest.
